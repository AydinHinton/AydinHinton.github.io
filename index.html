History of Video Games
Video games are digital games that can be played on a computer device (e.g., tablets, smartphones, laptops, consoles, etc.). Originally designed simply for entertainment, video games have grown into their own industry and field of learning, generating billions of dollars each year.

Video Games at Home (1960-1983)
  • In 1962, a man named steve russel at the massachusetts institute of technology invented a video game called spacewar!
  • The video gaming systems in the 1960’s were a lot simpler back then, and video games themselves were not complex, including a popular example called Pong.
  • In 1966, the first video game was invented by Ralph Baer, called Chase Game, where a pair of squares chased each other around a screen. In 1968 Baer created a prototype of a game called brown box, as well as game called Corndog, which was the first video game to be played on a television set.
